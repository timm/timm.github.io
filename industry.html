Notes on Industrial Collaboration

I have had much experience with collaboration between university and industrial/government organizations.

In all cases, the key to success was to take the time to learn the needs and social structures of the organizations.

For examples of this process, see below.

Verification of industrial processes

I am exploring, with an industrial partner (LexisNexis) issues related to the cost-effective exploration and deployment of models generated by data miners for Big Data applications.

This new lab, called “SeBig” (Software Engineering for Big Data) focuses on the validation of Big Data inference in industrial applications.

Initially, we are explore the certification and possible improvement of text mining methods for legal applications. Subsequent work will be adapted, according to earlier results. Research is conducted in an agile manner with a backlog of industrial-based problems being explored by graduate students in monthly sprints, monitored by faculty with daily scrums.

Research management

I worked for four years inside the firewalls of the NASA Software Assurance research program. There, I was a technical consultant to NASA civil servant as they decided how to allocated millions of dollars in research dollars to research centers.


Research programmers/ interns

In many cases, I have managed teams of graduate students performing programming tasks for industrial clients.

Two approaches for this are:

  • Term projects: During term time, my students often develop tools that implement cutting-edge algorithms for industrial clients. For example, for the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, my programmers have developed state-of-the-art effort estimation tools.
  • Summer interns: many of my students spend their summer working for industrial clients. When requested, I assist/guide in the management of those students (site visits, regular teleconfs).
